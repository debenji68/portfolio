Blox DIY — Homemade Nanoleaf

ESP32 · Microphone amp · WLED · Home Assistant · 3D Printing · Soldering

In production 100% homemade Sound-reactive

Building my own Nanoleaf-style LED panels from scratch. The project uses an ESP32 connected to a microphone amplifier to detect ambient sound and animate an ARGB LED strip in real time. WLED firmware is installed on the ESP32 and everything is integrated into Home Assistant for centralised control. The enclosures ("Blox") are modelled and 3D-printed. Electronic components are hand-soldered.

Features

💡 LED & Effects

  • Sound-reactive ARGB LED strip
  • Music-synchronised animations
  • 100+ visual effects via WLED
  • Full RGB colour control
  • Adjustable brightness and speed

🔌 Electronics

  • ESP32 — WiFi/BT microcontroller
  • Microphone amplifier for sound detection
  • Dedicated 5V power supply
  • Manual soldering of all components
  • Neat internal wiring

🏠 Home automation

  • Home Assistant integration via WLED
  • Control from mobile app
  • Automations (wake-up, ambiance, evening)
  • Scene and light groups
  • Compatible Google Home / Alexa via HA

🖨️ Manufacturing

  • "Blox" enclosure 3D modelling
  • FDM printing (PLA/PETG)
  • Integrated LED diffusers
  • Modular hexagonal design
  • Full mechanical assembly
